Generalities of Hyaluronic Acid:

Hyaluronic acid plays an essential role in the elasticity, volume and hydration of our skin. The hyaluronic acid is also widely spread in our body, in particular at the level of the articulations and the eyes, which confers him therapeutic uses in ophthalmology, rheumatology, orthopedics. Unfortunately, the natural quantity of hyaluronic acid decreases with age, which leads to the appearance of wrinkles on the skin. Waterloo hyaluronic acid injections fill the intercellular spaces in the dermis that are created, thus reducing the depth of wrinkles and increasing the skin's hydration.

Hyaluronic acid-based fillers are biocompatible and of non-animal origin. They are totally synthesized by genetic engineering and 100% biodegradable, the gel used in aesthetic medicine is injected into the superficial dermis or into the deep plane. Today, there is a wide range of products with different visco-elastic properties and volumizing powers. This allows the physician to choose the appropriate product according to the treatment indication (wrinkle filling or volumizing effect) and according to the patient's skin.

The effect of aging on facial volumes

From the age of 40 onwards, the loss of facial volume is mainly due to the natural phenomenon of skin aging and deep layers. The subcutaneous fat and bone structure of the face melt away, and the muscles relax, modifying the contours of the face. The temples, cheekbones and tear through become deeper, resulting in the appearance of nasolabial folds, jowls and bitterness folds. Wrinkles are therefore only a symptom of aging.

As we age (especially during menopause), the decrease in hormonal levels leads to less cell renewal. The upper layers of the skin produce less hyaluronic acid, resulting in a decrease in skin hydration.

At the same time, there is a reduction in elastin (the molecule responsible for the skin's elasticity), which has consequences on the thinness and sagging of the skin. The facial features then appear "drawn".

External factors can accelerate skin aging:

  • Prolonged exposure to the sun contributes to the deterioration of the collagen naturally present in the tissues as well as the structure of the skin.
  • Nicotine and the chemicals contained in tobacco also have a negative effect on collagen production in the skin, especially on the face.

Restoration of volumes by Hyaluronic Acid injections.

Depending on the area to be treated, the practitioner chooses a more or less cross-linked hyaluronic acid. The more the formula is cross-linked, the denser and more volumizing the product is. A highly cross-linked hyaluronic acid will be used to fill the cheekbones, and much less to plump up the mouth.

Here are the areas of the face that are particularly susceptible to volume loss:

  • Cheekbones (which lose their roundness)
  • The tear through
  • Dark circles (hollows in the periorbital area extending into the tear trough)
  • Cheeks (which become hollow)
  • The temples (which encourage the arch of the eyebrows to sag as they deepen)
  • The nasolabial folds
  • The marionnette lines
  • The oval and jowls
  • The chin and its region (which promote the formation of bitterness folds as they deepen)
  • The mouth (lips becoming thinner)
  • The nose (rarely, but likely to sag over time)

Please note that more than 90% of the areas of the face can be treated with hyaluronic acid injections.

Procedure for a hyaluronic acid injection session.

The deep planes are treated first, then the superficial planes.
Depending on the patient's skin type and the areas to be treated, the injections can be done with a needle or with micro-cannulas. The product is injected slowly and precisely, with a good knowledge of the anatomy. A single area or the entire treatment can be done in one session, depending on the patient's wishes. The duration of the session varies from 15 to 50 minutes. The results are immediately visible, the volumes reappear and the contours are redrawn. The session ends with a light massage of the treated areas with a soothing cream. Make-up can be applied immediately afterwards if needed.

Medical peelings associated with an LED session

The result of hyaluronic acid injections is visible immediately, unlike other techniques (laser, peeling...). However, the result is optimal 10-15 days after the session.

A little redness or slight swelling may be observed in the injected area. These side effects fade after a few hours, and bruising may occur, but large hematomas are rare. A good covering make-up is useful in this case for a few days.

Sometimes a small lump can be felt when touching the skin for 48 hours, the time it takes for the hyaluronic acid to be homogeneously incorporated into the dermis and the deeper layers.

Finally, the risks of allergy are exceptional (the product is not of animal origin).
Like many medical techniques for facial rejuvenation, hyaluronic acid allows patients to resume their daily activities immediately after the procedure. The only precautions to take are to avoid sauna, hammam, and sun exposure for at least 3 days.

Hyaluronic acid is naturally and slowly resorbed in the body. Therefore, the results will gradually fade with time. The durability of the results depends on the type of product used, the quantity injected, and the mobility and depth of the treated area.

On average, the effects last from 6 to 12 months, and even 18 months. It is therefore necessary to perform regular sessions to maintain a good level of maintenance, especially during the first 2 years of facial aging treatment.
