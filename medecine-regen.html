Regenerative medicine / PRP

How does regenerative medicine work ?

PRP (platelet rich plasma) injections are used in the traditional medical field to treat painful tendonitis, ligament, hip, knee, Achilles heel, muscle pain, knee osteoarthritis and many other conditions.

The injection of PRP or "Vampire Lift" is now a common practice in aesthetic medicine.

The platelet concentrate stimulates the cells with which it comes into contact. PRP allows for better vascularization, regeneration of skin tissue, remodeling of your skin, and boosting your collagen production. It allows for skin rejuvenation, and limits hair loss. Plasma or PRP injections can treat: face, neck, scalp, and cleavage...

Course of a consultation

The procedure is quite simple. First, a blood sample will be taken. Once the sample is taken, the blood is centrifuged. The purpose of this centrifugation is to isolate the platelet-enriched plasma. The collected PRP will be reinjected with a syringe into the area you wish to treat. Each session lasts on average between 45 and 60 minutes and is not painful.

Areas of the body that can be treated

In my clinic of aesthetic medicine in Waterloo, I perform PRP injections under perfect hygienic conditions. The different areas of the body that can be treated are :

the face

Scalp

  • Prevention of incipient baldness
  • Acceleration of regrowth after a hair transplant
Secondary effects

Possible side effects following a PRP injection are mainly tingling at the injection sites, redness or some pain that can be treated with painkillers such as paracetamol or anti-inflammatory drugs. I will give you all the necessary advice for your health and well-being after the injection.

Contraindications

Contraindications are also possible in certain cases, for example skin cancers and certain skin diseases. Many questions will be asked during the preliminary interview in order to verify your medical history and not to take any unnecessary risks.
